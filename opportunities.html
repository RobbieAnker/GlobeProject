Buy Sustainably

The things people buy have contributed to climate change enormously. If people stop buying unsustainable items, companies will stop producing them.

The United Nation's Food and Agriculture Organisation (FAO) found that 14.5% of all our greenhouse gas emissions come from livestock. And, cattle raised for both beef and milk makes up 65% of the greenhouse gas emissions from livestock. You can read more about this at http://fao.org.

For example, you can do your part by making informed decisions on what food and drink you buy, what clothes you buy, and what restaurants you go to.

There is plenty of information available to help you buy sustainably. One website is https://mygreencloset.com/ which can help you decide what clothes to buy.



↓

How You Vote

Some governments have proven much more effective than others at tackling climate change. For the level of change that is required to be achieved, there has to be changes from the top. The world leaders must take on their responsibility to care for the people they govern, and for the planet their people depend on.

A great opportunity to create positive change is voting in a general election. If you want a leader that will fight climate change, vote for one that you trust to do so.



↓

How You Travel

Transportation has contributed to a very significant amount of climate change. The European Environment Agency reported that, in 2017, 27% of Europe's greenhouse gas emissions came from transport.

One way you can help is by using public transport when possible, especially those powered by electricity which are mostly rail services.



↓

Minimise Waste

Minimising the amount of waste you produce can be instrumental in our fight for a harmonious planet.

To help out you can recycle, and even avoid purchasing items with any packaging items when possible. You could for example visit thezerowaster.com/zero-waste-near-you to see what shops near you produce no waste by letting you bring your own containers for the items you purchase. The more people that shop like this, the more that shops will change to meet the demand for no waste.

Your Job

Not everyone is in a position to decide what their job is, and the most important thing is to put food on your table and a roof over your head.

However, there are many companies that are looking to move towards a greener world. There are 10 examples of global companies that are environmentally friendly in an article by Virgin, and available at:
https://www.virgin.com/virgin-unite/10-global-companies-are-environmentally-friendly.

Volunteer

Lots of great work is being done by volunteers, and is a good chance for you to make a difference if you are eager and have some spare time.

One quick way of seeing what environmental volunteering opportunities are out there is by visiting https://environmentjob.co.uk.

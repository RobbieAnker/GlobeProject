Europe

Nearly 1,500 people killed last year by hottest summer on record



↓

More Details

In the summer of 2019 there were 2 heat waves in Europe – the first in June and the second in July.

The June heat wave killed 567 people, the July heat wave killed 868. A total of 1,435 deaths.

The following countries experienced their hottest temperatures on record that 2019 summer:

  • Belgium
  • France
  • Germany
  • Luxembourg
  • The Netherlands
  • United Kingdom



↓

An Impact of Climate Change

Climate change has made heat waves, and therefore tragic events like this, more likely.

If the emission of greenhouse gases continues, climate change will continue, and its effects will eventually become far more extreme than they have been so far.

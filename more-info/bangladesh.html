Bangladesh

13.3 million people's homes to be in ruins by 2050



↓

More Details

In 30 years from now, 13.3 million citizens of Bangladesh may be displaced due to their homes being irreparably damaged.

Bangladesh is particularly vulnerable due to being naturally prone to extreme weather such as flooding and cyclones. In 2009 the cyclone Aila killed more than 200 people.

The country's coastal position and its low elevation from sea level also enhance its vulnerability, as they increase the risk of flooding. Some scientists have predicted that, if the rate of greenhouse gas emissions doesn't slow down, the global sea level will rise by over 5 feet by 2100, which could displace 50 million citizens of Bangladesh.



↓

An Impact of Climate Change

Global sea level rise has already caused an increase of flooding in Bangladesh. This includes making the flooding which results from cyclones even more devastating.

One way that the warming climate has caused this is by melting glaciers and ice sheets. Also, the sea water has expanded due to its increased temperature.

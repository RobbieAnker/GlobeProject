The Sahara Desert

The Sahara desert has grown by 10%



↓

More Details

The Sahara desert has expanded by 10% between 1920 to 2013.

The Sahara is expanding into the region of Sahel, disrupting the fragile grassland ecosystems and societies of the region.



↓

An Impact of Climate Change

The warming climate may have aided the expansion of the Sahara by altering atmospheric circulation patterns such as the Hadley cell, which is the process of hot air rising at the equator, moving outward towards the poles until it gets to the subtropics, at which point it descends and then travels back towards the equator to repeat the cycle.

This air in this cycle being hotter than usual could cause deserts such as the Sahara to expand.

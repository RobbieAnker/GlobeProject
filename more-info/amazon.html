Amazon Rainforest

The Amazon Rainforest is set to be wiped out in 100 years



↓

More Details

If deforestation continues at its current rate, the Amazon will be destroyed, for purposes such as cattle ranching, within 100 years.

Most of this deforestation happens illegally.

The cattle sector is responsible for 80% of the Amazon's deforestation to support the beef and leather trades.



↓

Impact on Climate Change

This would cause climate change to take place even more rapidly, as the plant life in the Amazon currently absorbs 2 billion tons of CO2 every year.

This makes the Amazon's survival crucial to the planet's wellbeing, as CO2 the main greenhouse gas responsible for climate change.
